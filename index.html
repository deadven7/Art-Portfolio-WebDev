An Afternoon



An Afternoon


I look up, see a vision never seen
Through the tranquil setting shone a smile
Like a ripple through the waters serene
My heart stopped beating there for a while.
As I mustered the courage to open my mouth
I let out a little whisper, “you came back”
“I had never left", and as my brain began to shout
A glimmer of hope, through a life pitch black.


“Where have you been?” “Where did you go?”
“I was at my place, woke up just now”
“After all this time, now you show?”
“I would never leave you, I made that vow.”
I looked into those eyes, saw my face
An expression of horror, with a tiny smile
My heart beat through my ears at a high pace
Staring, wondering if it's a subtle guile.


I talked and talked, knowing not to stop
Told about my day, talked about the obvious
Nothing beside me, no sound heard in that swamp
At a barista in the market, without an audience.
Saw a chuckle that lit up my day
Heard a sound that I've been longing to hear
Felt a presence that I wanted to stay
Not wanting to lose it, not wanting that fear.


As I'm laughing my lungs out, I feel a tap
I turn and see a man, looked a little scared
“Who are you talking to, sir?”, asked the old chap
“Can’t you see?” I looked at the table, no one was there.
Everything is back to normal, back to sad
Absence of my beloved, after all these years
Embarrassment, those memories would drive me mad
The one life that mattered, that went away, was hers.

The Room



The Room


A dark night, sky was black
A man walking with a bag on his back
Walking unhindered through the lonely road
In search of a place to stay, a humble abode.
An actor by profession, about to shoot in wilderness
Nothing for miles in sight he was stressed
All he saw was a motel at the far end
He ran up to it, asking if they had a room to lend.
The lady obliged with a room, the last one left
Talking wonders but of the flaws she was bereft
Keen to have him as the customer, she gave the key
Unknown of the sinisters that made everyone flee.


He drops the bag on the floor and falls on the bed
Lights were off, door was closed, he lay there dead
The curtains moved with a rustle, the door began to creak,
Is all of this a coincidence? Chances seemed bleak.
It was unusual, the sounds, but he paid no heed
Until red dripped from the walls, they seemed to bleed
He ran to the reception helter-skelter​
As pale as milk, face completely flustered.
The lady had no room, he had to spend another day,
He went back up in vain, here's where he had to stay,
The room seemed haunted, it could be chance,
But if he stayed anymore, he'd be in a trance.


It was the last night, he had endured quite a few
The moon was scary enough, did it have to be blue?
He stayed up till he slept, but all in vain
The winds began to move, it began to rain.
A loud thud on the door, he jolted up afraid
It kept banging, like a fool he didn't stay
Walked up to the door, fear inbred
There was nothing, all he could see was bright red.
The next morning, he began on his journey
Began a chat with the coolie, interested was he
The coolie described the room, through the story he sped,
“Oh but one last thing sir, the ghost's eyes were red”.

Seaside


A beautiful morning, sky was awfully bright
Tranquility in its nature, filled with a blinding light
An endless canvas, pure and uninterrupting blue
Like an unending sea, with a tinge of turquoise hue.
The waves were crawling towards the shore
Buzzing with the dormant strength they bore
Slumbering in its blue robe, appealing to our serenity
Swollen to its confined depths, filling the air with solemnity.
The horizon seemed to be stitched with a silver line
Laminated by the Sun did the glistening sand shine
Golden in the truest sense, the waves frothed against the beach
Gateway to paradise, the opera of the waves preached.


A sudden dance of the torrid clouds filled the sky
Changed to a dark grey as cold winds blew by
The waved turned fierce, bellowing with all their might
A misty fog engulfed the scene, dimming our sight.
The sky changed colours, succeeded by a thundering roar
Bright flashes of light struck down to the floor
The wind began to howl, like a wolf in the night
The waves showed their strength, adding terror to our fright.
Black clouds billowed in from everywhere
The sky coupled with the sea were a scare
The rains made the waves escalate unreachable heights
It was a dark sky, but it shone through the lights.

Conflict


Rolls of waves dressed the shore
Sand, glistening under a weakened Sun
Nothing seen for miles down to the horizon
Solitarily lying in a commoner utopia.
A tale of fiction, visited in fables
A search for an optimistic runaway
That's where I stood, an escape from reality
At the ends of the Earth, the end of the run.


A reflection in myself, as I looked on
The calm serene waters turned
Into a tumultuous war within itself
A conflicted direction, or so it seemed.
Crashing onto each other, they killed each other
A ruthless fashion, a swift swipe over the procession
One leaping jump, and suddenly all gone
In the end, limping onto the shore.


My gaze, stuck on the warring waves
Almost lost focus on the surrounding nature
An acoustic calling, playful birds floating the skies
As the pregnant clouds drifted from the Sun.
The beach, a shining diamond
Covered in a transparent tranquility
As a divided me looked on, with undead eyes
A peace within nature, rested the conflict within me.

Unstable Mind

Among the bones and hearts that broke
Surrounded by rubble and blood and tears
Found himself amidst man's greatest fear.

Neither of the wind nor of the bird
Couldn't see clear, couldn't stand proper

Eyes that bulge out, life was sucked out

Leaned to the side, a newspaper that read

Typed in from the blood their wounds bleed.
He sat to wonder if the triumph was true
They won no prize, the victory had no clue
Mental scars were all that was left behind
A torture for eternity, for an unstable mind.

Fire and Frost

One Last Message


Okay, I say my piece for the last time
As I peek through the blinders at the shining Sun
A ray shone in my room like a glimmer of hope
Illuminating the dark room where I was shunned.
Standing in the middle of nothing, I look around
Sun shone on me like a spotlight, I was prepared
I turned to the mirror, then straight ahead
Without a doubt in my mind, my hand was steady.


A narration, a story, an essay that spoke my thought
A dysfunctional society centred around my antics
A tale of heroism, a scrawny nerd who fought
A relentless battle against the bullies and haters.
It was my message to the world, a vow of silence broken
The ripples were loud, till it backfired on me
I sat there, taken apart bit by bit by scavengers
It was their turn, I could either fight back or be a coward.


I realised I poked the bear, and a mistake I can't undo
The warzone was brutal, as they did not end it
Hitting every nerve, targeting every point till all sense was gone
As I looked, the comments kept adding up, they just did not know when to stop.
From the body shaming to the personal misdemeanor
From the family jokes to threats on my life
A skull reflected in the tears that fell down my face
I turned off the computer, nothing else I could do.


I feared switching it on, didn't know what would happen
But my mother saw my face on the internet
I was everywhere, something I didn't want or ask
From memes to trolls, I looked in disbelief.
My face was on deceased reports, on kidnaps of kids
It became difficult to leave my house unseen
As my frowns met their laughter and their horror
That was it, I could take it no more, it had to cease.


Locked in my own home, lost the trust of my own family
A comment, that ruined everyone’s life
I made my decision, as I send this message, one last comment
Never pick a fight you can't hope to win.
With that I switched off the camera, and looked at the blade
Wedged between my fingers, one motion with swiftness
I opened my eyes, and all I saw was red,
But it was okay, because I was back to the old darkness.
